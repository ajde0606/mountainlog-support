ClimbLog Support & Help Center

Welcome to the official ClimbLog support page. ClimbLog is a SwiftUI iOS climbing journal designed to capture your days out with photos, voice notes, and AI climbing trip reports. It uses a free, Groq-powered engine for fast, natural-language summaries and is built to be offline-friendly so you can log beta even when you’re far from service.

On this page you’ll find iOS climbing journal help, setup guides, privacy details, and troubleshooting steps for AI reports, Mountain Project imports, and WhisperKit transcription offline.

Getting Started with ClimbLog

ClimbLog is designed to make it fast and fun to capture every pitch, crag day, or big objective with rich context. A single “entry” can contain multiple media pairs (photo + voice note) and optional Mountain Project route data.

Creating Your First Climb Entry

  • Open ClimbLog and tap “New Climb” (or the + button) on the main journal screen.
  • Give the session a title (e.g., “Red Rock – Dark Shadows” or “Yosemite Valley Weekend”).
  • Optionally, add notes or tags (crag, partner name, weather, grade) so you can search for this day later.

Adding Multiple Photo / Voice Pairs

Each climb entry can hold multiple media pairs so you can capture a pitch-by-pitch story:

  • Tap “Add Photo & Voice Note” within an entry.
  • Choose or capture a photo (lead shot, anchor, topo, or gear spread). Vertical and horizontal photos are both supported.
  • Tap the record button to add a voice note with beta, feelings, or context.
  • ClimbLog automatically keeps each photo + audio pair together so your AI trip reports can follow the story sequence.

Capturing Images

  • Grant camera and photo library access when prompted.
  • Use the in-app camera to snap quick shots, or import from your existing photo roll.
  • For best results, capture key moments (start of climb, crux, summit, anchors) that will make AI summaries more vivid.

Recording Locally Transcribed Audio

When you record a voice note, ClimbLog uses Whisper/WhisperKit-based transcription locally on your device. Audio is stored on-device and transcribed to text without leaving your phone.

  • Tap the record button and speak naturally (beta, emotions, weather, rack, partner banter, etc.).
  • Stop recording when you’re done; ClimbLog will transcribe the note into searchable text.
  • You can edit the transcription manually if you want to clean up names or grades for future reference.

Pulling Mountain Project Route Data

To make logs more structured, ClimbLog can attach Mountain Project route data to your entries:

  • Search or paste a route name exactly as listed on Mountain Project (e.g., “Physical Graffiti (5.6)”).
  • ClimbLog will attempt to fetch route details such as grade, style (trad/sport/toprope), pitches, and location.
  • If route matching fails, you can still log climbs manually and note Mountain Project URLs in your text notes.

AI-Powered Trip Reports

ClimbLog uses a Groq API-powered model to turn your photos, voice notes, and route metadata into rich AI climbing trip reports. Reports typically generate in about 2–8 seconds, depending on your connection and how much content is in the entry.

Setting Up the Groq API

  • Create a free account at the Groq developer site and generate an API key.
  • In ClimbLog, open Settings > AI Trip Reports.
  • Paste your Groq API key into the designated field.
  • Test the connection by tapping “Generate Test Report” (if available) or creating a short sample entry and running AI.

Generating a Trip Report

  • Finish adding media and notes for your climb entry.
  • Tap “Generate AI Trip Report” in the entry view.
  • ClimbLog sends only the necessary text and meta data (transcriptions, route info, tags) to the Groq model for summarization.
  • Within a few seconds, you’ll see a narrative report that you can review, edit, and copy into your journal, blog, or social posts.

Tips for Rich Narratives

  • Include emotional context in your voice notes (fear, stoke, fatigue, send attempts).
  • Mention weather, conditions, and rack choices so the AI can highlight them.
  • Add a quick “overall story” voice note at the end of the day to help the model stitch everything together.
  • Use consistent naming (e.g., “Pitch 1: …”, “Pitch 2: …”) across notes for multi-pitch routes.

Privacy & Offline Capabilities

ClimbLog is built with privacy-first and offline-friendly design in mind. Your climbing memories belong to you.

On-Device Storage

  • All entries, photos, and voice notes are stored locally on your device.
  • Data is not uploaded to a server or sold to third parties.
  • You can delete individual entries at any time, or remove the app to clear stored data (subject to iOS backup settings).

WhisperKit Transcription Offline

ClimbLog uses WhisperKit (or a similar Whisper-based engine) to transcribe your audio notes. This is designed so voice recordings never need to leave your phone for transcription.

  • The transcription model is downloaded once and then runs locally.
  • You can record and transcribe even when you are fully offline.
  • Only when you explicitly request an AI trip report does text content leave your device (to the Groq API).

AI & Network Usage

Note: AI trip reports require an internet connection to talk to the Groq API. You can log everything in airplane mode and generate reports later once you’re back online.

Troubleshooting Guides

If ClimbLog isn’t working as expected, use the quick checks below for common issues like camera/microphone access, Whisper model downloads, Mountain Project route import issues, or Groq API errors.

Camera Permissions

  • On iOS, go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera.
  • Ensure ClimbLog is toggled On.
  • Restart ClimbLog and try adding a new photo.

Microphone & Audio Recording

  • Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Microphone.
  • Confirm ClimbLog is allowed to use the microphone.
  • If recordings fail, force-quit the app, reopen it, and attempt a short 1–2 second test recording.

Whisper / WhisperKit Model Download Issues

  • Make sure you have a stable Wi-Fi connection when the model is first downloaded.
  • Keep the app open until the initial download finishes.
  • If transcription fails consistently, try restarting the device and triggering a fresh download from within ClimbLog’s settings (if available).

Mountain Project Route Fetching Problems

If you experience Mountain Project route import issues:

  • Double-check spelling, grade format, and location in your search.
  • Try including more context, such as the area or wall name in the search text.
  • If a route still can’t be matched, log it manually and paste the Mountain Project link into your notes for reference.

Groq API Errors

  • Verify that your Groq API key is valid and not expired or revoked.
  • Check for extra spaces when pasting the key into ClimbLog settings (especially at the end of the string).
  • Ensure you have an active internet connection; AI trip reports won’t work in airplane mode or offline.
  • If the Groq service is rate-limited or temporarily unavailable, wait a few minutes and try again.

FAQs & Future Enhancements

How many media pairs can I add to a single climb?

ClimbLog is designed to support multiple photo/voice pairs per entry so you can document each pitch or moment. Practical limits are determined by your device storage; if you’re low on space, consider trimming unused photos or audio clips.

Does ClimbLog work offline?

Yes. You can create entries, add photos, record voice notes, and run WhisperKit transcription offline. Only AI trip report generation and Mountain Project route fetching require connectivity.

Can I export or back up my data?

Future versions of ClimbLog are planned to include more export options (e.g., text/markdown export, JSON, or CSV summaries). For now, you can manually copy AI reports and notes into your own documents or journals.

What future features are planned?

We’re actively exploring improvements based on user feedback, including:

  • GPS tagging of entries and pitches.
  • Apple Watch support to quickly capture notes at belays.
  • More flexible data export and backup options.
  • Additional AI templates (e.g., gear lists, condition summaries, partner shout-outs).

These ideas help ClimbLog rank for long-tail queries like “AI climbing trip reports with GPS tags” and “Apple Watch iOS climbing journal help” while shaping the future roadmap.
